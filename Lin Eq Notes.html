Constants, as the word might suggest, refers to a Constant value. What this means is that it has a Specific value and cannot change. So, numbers like 2, -1, 3, 10, are all Constants
On the other hand, Algebra also deals with terms called Variables. This refers to its ability to vary. They are usually denoted with Alphabets (x,y,n) OR Symbols(#,$,^). If they vary, it means that its value can change in different scenarios. For Example-
If a number, say X, is added to 7 to make it 8, What would it be? One, Of course. But if instead of this, it was -
If a number, say X, is added to 7 to make it 9, What would it be? Two, in this case. So, it has the ability to vary, unlike constants.

In an Algebraic Expression, a combination of Variables and Constants is operated upon with different operations like-
Addition {+}
Subtraction {-}
Multiplication {×}
Division {÷}
