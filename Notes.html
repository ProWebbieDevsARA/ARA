Introduction to the world of Rational Numbers

You may have heard people saying-"Do you know that 1 is a Rational Number.". Well, today let's understand what are Rational Numbers. When you speak the word "Rational", you may get a hint of what it may mean.

Number           Fractional Form          Rational Number?
7                            7/1                                  Yes!
3                            3/1                                  Yes!
0                            0/1                                  Yes!
π               22/7 (Closest! Not exact)                No! As there is no Definite Value

What i mean by this is just that whenever we represent Rational Numbers, we represent it in a fractional form. In that as well, rules apply. The numeratpr is given the name of P and the denominator is called Q. These denote the values in the numerator and the denominator. When i say P and Q are integers, I mean that they can be either Negative or Positive numbers and not fractions. Q will be another integer as well. But it comes with an added arguement that it can't be equal to 0, unlike P which can be. Any number divided by itself is 1 so if a fraction like 6/10 was to be divided by 1, we wud still consider that as a rational number. Why? You might think that the numerator is a fraction so it would not be. But, like I told before, any number divided by itself is one. So when you do that, the number you will get is 6/10, and this would fulfill the conditions for making it a rational number.
